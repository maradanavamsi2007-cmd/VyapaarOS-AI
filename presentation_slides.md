## Slide 2: The Problem
**Headline:** The Fragmented Reality of Indian Kirana Operations

* **The Challenges:**
  1. **Fragmented Apps:** Separate tools for bookkeeping, billing, inventory, and marketing.
  2. **Manual Overhead:** Inputting paper invoices and matching tax details takes hours.
  3. **High Customer Attrition:** No mechanism to identify "at-risk" customers or recover outstanding credit (*udhaar*).
  4. **Poor Supply Chains:** Late restocking leading to stockouts of high-frequency items (like milk).
* **Visual Direction:** A split screen showing a cluttered shop counter full of receipts next to a stack of isolated mobile apps (Billing, Excel, WhatsApp).
* **Presenter Notes:**
  > Walk into any Indian Kirana store. You see paper ledgers, a calculator, and WhatsApp groups. Owners are drowning in fragmented apps and manual work. They have no visibility on cash flow, lose customers to supermarkets, and suffer stockouts.

## Slide 5: Voice-First AI & Operations Agent
**Headline:** Multilingual Speech Commands & Autonomous Procurement

* **Voice-First Integration:**
  * Speak commands naturally in Hinglish ("Aaj Ramesh ko ₹1500 udhaar diya") or Telugu ("ఈ రోజు పాలు అయిపోతున్నాయి").
  * System interprets intent, updates the database, and responds via Text-to-Speech (TTS).
* **Decision Engine POs:**
  * Monitored stock levels auto-trigger Purchase Order drafts.
  * Displays clear reasons (e.g., "Dairy milk packets at 3 left, minimum limit is 25").
* **Visual Direction:** Glowing concentric audio rings representing voice capture; horizontal stepper showing the procurement timeline.
* **Presenter Notes:**
  > Shop owners are busy. They can speak directly to VyapaarOS in their native language—Telugu, Hindi, or Hinglish. Saying "Ramesh ko 1500 udhaar diya" instantly parses the ledger without typing. When stock runs low, the Operations Agent prepares a PO draft, waiting for a single click to approve.

## Slide 8: Under the Hood - Technology Stack
**Headline:** Fast, Portable, and Scalable

* **Frontend:** React + Vite, Tailwind CSS, Lucide icons, HTML5 Canvas Particle networks.
* **Backend:** Node.js, Express, RESTful APIs, Local fallback JSON database for instant offline usage.
* **APIs & SDKs:** Web Speech API (speech-to-text / text-to-speech), Meta WhatsApp Cloud API / wa.me redirection.
* **Deployment:** Concurrent monorepo build deployed on Vercel.
* **Visual Direction:** Minimalist stack diagram highlighting frontend, backend, APIs, and the fallback database layers.
* **Presenter Notes:**
  > Our technology stack is designed for speed. We use React with a high-performance 2D canvas network, Node/Express, and the browser's Web Speech API. If internet connectivity drops, the local database fallback ensures operations keep running smoothly.
